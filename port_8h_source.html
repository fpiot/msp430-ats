134 /* Analysis of port capabilities:
135  *
136  * 1xx: P1/P2 are uniform contiguous with interrupt capability. P3-P6
137  * are uniform contiguous without interrupt capability. There is no
138  * REN capability.
139  *
140  * 2xx: P1/P2 are uniform contiguous inclusive of REN but exclusive of
141  * SEL2, which is contiguous at a distinct base address, and have
142  * interrupt capability. P3-P6 are uniform 8-bit and P7-P8 are
143  * uniform 16-bit contiguous, exclusive of REN and SEL2. If any port
144  * has SEL2, all ports have SEL2. PA=(P7,P8) in the headers, but is
145  * not named as such in the user's guide.
146  *
147  * 3xx: P1/P2 are uniform contiguous with interrupt capability. P3-P4
148  * are uniform contiguous without interrupt capability. There is no
149  * REN capability.
150  *
151  * 4xx: P1/P2 are uniform contiguous including REN capability, and
152  * have interrupt capability. P3-P6 are uniform 8-bit and P7-P10 are
153  * uniform 16-bit contiguous; these are exclusive of REN, which is
154  * contiguous at a distinct base address. Some MCUs have SEL2 on P1,
155  * but no other ports have SEL2. PA=(P7,P8), PB=(P9,P10).
156  *
157  * 5xx: All ports uniform 16-bit contiguous. PA=(P1,P2), PB=(P3,P4),
158  * and so forth. All registers have space reserved for interrupt
159  * support. Only FR5XX has PxSELC.
160  *
161  * In the above, "8-bit" means that the register fields are
162  * immediately adjacent with byte addresses. "16-bit" means that
163  * register pairs are interleaved within function; e.g. P1IN would be
164  * at 0x00 while P2IN is at 0x01 and P1OUT at 0x02.
165  *
166  * 16-bit ports were introduced in MSP430X and are available on
167  * several chips in the 2xx and 4xx family. In 2xx/4xx MCUs, PORTA
168  * overlays P7/P8; in 5xx MCUs it overlays P1/P2.
169  *
170  * In pre-5xx families, registers supporting interrupts have a
171  * different layout than registers that do not support interrupts.
172  * However, the layouts (exclusive of SEL2 and REN) are compatible
173  * within those types.
174  *
175  * Thus there are several distinct layouts that need to be supported:
176  *
177  * + [PORT_IE_8] Pre-5xx interrupt-capable, 8-bit interface
178  * + [PORT_8] Pre-5xx non-interrupt, 8-bit interface
179  * + [PORT_16] Pre-5xx non-interrupt, 16-bit interface
180  * + [PORT_5XX] 5xx registers, 8-bit interface
181  * + [PORT_5XX_16] 5xx registers, 16-bit interface
182  *
183  * 5xx chips are identified by MSP430XV2_CPU. For these, a uniform
184  * HPL based on the 16-bit alphabetic register names is presented.
185  */
